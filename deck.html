The problem

Every BMW owner wants flames. Nobody has a good way to get them.

Tune the car's computer — $1,500 to $4,200

You pay to unlock the computer, buy a tuning licence, then add expensive exhaust hardware. It stresses the engine, risks the warranty — and on a modern BMW, you still mostly get crackle, not a real torch.

Buy a cheap bolt-on kit — $60 to $220

These kits work by starving the engine of a spark. A modern BMW detects this in milliseconds and shuts off the fuel. They simply never fire. People waste the money and nothing happens.

Result: BMW owners either spend thousands for crackle, or waste money on kits that do nothing. There is no clean answer — until now.

The solution

We bypass both problems entirely.

Flame Link injects its own fuel and fires its own spark at the exhaust tip — completely separate from the engine. The car's computer never knows it's there. The engine stays exactly as the factory built it.
