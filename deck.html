The problem

Flames are the most-wanted BMW mod. They're also the hardest to get.

BMW owners spend big on looks and sound, and flames are the headline. But on a modern BMW, there is no good way to get them.

The tune route — $1,500 to $4,200

You unlock the locked ECU, buy a tuning licence, and add a downpipe and intercooler. It reflashes your engine, voids the warranty — and still only crackles, not a real flame.

The cheap kits — don't work

Hot Licks and the $60–$220 kits spark into raw fuel the engine has to dump. A modern BMW cuts that fuel the instant it sees a misfire, so on an F/G they simply never fire.

The solution

We put the fuel and the spark in the exhaust — not the engine.

  • Standalone. It makes its own flame at the exhaust tip.
  • Automatic. It reads the car's data bus and fires on lift-off and downshift. No button.
  • Engine-safe. It never touches the ignition, injectors, or ECU. Fully reversible.
  • Tunable. You set the flames from your phone.

One price: $650. Installed, about $800.
