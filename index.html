Why Flame Link works on BMWs

Other kits don't work here.

Most bolt-on flame kits cut your ignition to create a spark and dump fuel into the cylinders. That thins your oil, stresses your rings, and doesn't even work on modern BMWs.

We know — we tried. The BMW DME is smarter than that. The moment it detects a missing ignition event, it cuts fuel injection entirely. No fuel, no flame. Those kits simply can't fire on a BMW.

Flame Link is different. It's a fully standalone exhaust system. It never touches your ignition, your injectors, or your DME. It injects fuel and sparks in the exhaust tip, downstream of everything. The ECU doesn't know it exists — and your engine runs exactly as BMW built it.

We're a tiny automotive engineering team based in a small town in Ghana — been researching and testing on our own 428i for the longest. This isn't a theory. This is what works.

Questions

Before you ask.

Will it hurt my engine or reliability?

No. Flame Link is fully standalone — it works in the exhaust, downstream of your engine, and never touches your internals, timing or factory tune. Your motor runs exactly as the factory built it, and the kit comes off without a trace.

Why not just get a flame tune instead?

A flame tune reflashes your ECU and forces flames by dumping fuel and retarding timing — that piles heat and stress onto your engine, can hit your warranty and resale, costs more, and isn't easy to undo. Flame Link makes the same flames without any of that: a bolt-on that leaves your engine 100% stock. Same show, none of the risk.

Is it safe?

It's built around safety: hard burn-time limits, a hardware watchdog and fused power, and it cuts every output the instant something's off. High-grade, heat-rated parts and a sealed gasoline feed — no open fuel.

What fuel does it use?

A small, sealed gasoline feed. The lines stay closed — nothing leaks, drips or pools, on a hot exhaust or anywhere else.

Will it flame on its own, or catch fire?

Only when you trigger it — a lift-off, a downshift, or the app. Parked with the engine off it's completely inert; there's no open flame sitting there waiting.

Which cars does it fit?

The kit itself is universal — it lives on the exhaust, not tied to any one engine. Today the software is dialled in for BMW F & G series (with a plug-in harness), and we're rolling out support for more platforms. Drop your model in the order form and we'll tell you where it stands and ping you when yours is ready.

Can I control how big the flames are?

Yes. Tune flame size, intensity and crackle from the app, save your setups and switch on the fly — from a subtle daily crackle to full send.

Is it legal?

Off-road and show use only. A flame kit may be illegal on public roads where you live — check your local laws and run it responsibly.

How is it installed?

It ships with the wiring harness and a step-by-step guide. Want it done for you? Professional installation by our team is GH₵ 2,000 — add it in the order form.
